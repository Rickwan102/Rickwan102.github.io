Three projects, one skillset

Each project demonstrates a different facet of applied data science — from machine learning and deep learning to AI-powered application development.

IST 707 — Applied Machine Learning
Mental Health & Wellbeing Analysis: Predicting Anxiety and Depression Among Syracuse Students
Analyzed responses from 4,957 Syracuse University students in the 2025 Campus Wellness Survey to predict anxiety and depression using machine learning. Built Random Forest classifiers achieving 72.55% accuracy for anxiety and 79.04% for depression, then used DBSCAN and HDBSCAN clustering to segment students into distinct intervention profiles. Key finding: loneliness was the single strongest predictor of both conditions across all models, with lonely students showing 2–5× higher rates of mental health issues compared to socially connected peers.
Stakeholder
Syracuse University Barnes Center for Health & Wellbeing
Actionable insight
Identified high-risk student clusters to support proactive, data-informed outreach — shifting from reactive crisis management
Technologies
Python scikit-learn Random Forest DBSCAN HDBSCAN t-SNE

Deep Learning Course
Music Recommendations for Indie Artists Using Deep Learning
Built a Convolutional Autoencoder that analyzes short 5-second audio clips to identify acoustically similar songs — bridging mainstream and independent music catalogs. Audio data from 8,000 Free Music Archive tracks was converted into mel-spectrograms and split into 83,290 overlapping snippets for training. The model learns 256-dimensional embeddings in a latent space and achieved 70% accuracy in identifying the correct song as one of the most similar tracks, with a final validation loss of 0.0042 MSE.
Goal
Help indie artists gain exposure through meaningful audio similarity matching on short clips (as heard on TikTok/Instagram)
Actionable insight
A 256-dimensional audio embedding model that correctly identifies similar tracks 70% of the time from 5-second clips
Technologies
Python PyTorch Convolutional Autoencoder Mel-spectrograms Free Music Archive

AI Application Development
AI Tax Assistant: RAG-Powered Chatbot for Federal Tax Guidance
Developed a full-stack AI chatbot that answers tax questions using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) over a custom IRS document vector database. Built on Google Gemini 2.5 Pro and LangChain, the application processes real tax documents (W-2s, 1099s) via OCR and PDF parsing, automatically detects and masks personally identifiable information (PII) to protect user privacy, maintains session memory across conversations, and generates personalized tax filing checklists based on each user's profile. Deployed as a Streamlit application.
Actionable insight
Personalized, real-time tax guidance grounded in IRS documents — accessible to users without tax expertise
Ethics highlight
Automatic PII detection & masking; legal disclaimer; sessions are user-controlled and deletable
Technologies
Python Streamlit LangChain Google Gemini RAG ChromaDB EasyOCR
